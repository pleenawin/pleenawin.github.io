The usual questions

At the Museum of Dream Space in Los Angeles. Photo credit: Daniel Shot That Website | Instagram

I’m sorry, what was your name?

Hey there! You know, for some reason, people often think I say “Helena,” but my name is Paulina. Sorry for the confusion.

What do you do?

I started out as an intern for an interactive design team at an ad agency in Louisiana. Now I focus on designing interfaces for an online marketplace in California. I enjoy working closely with other disciplines, and learning from them to help me become a better designer/employee/teammate. I also help maintain brand standards, manage projects, and coordinate events, among other things.

What are your priorities when designing?

In most scenarios, I prioritize the following:

  1. Clarity – Text is legible/digestible, calls to action are clear, outcomes are predictable
  2. Simplicity – Content displayed is relevant, icons/graphics add value
  3. Responsiveness – Page load time is reasonable for all internet speeds

Of course, striking a balance between these priorities depends on the project since every project has different goals, audience, and contents.

Do you have any interests outside of design?

I enjoy puzzles (e.g. jigsaw, Tetris, fitting items in luggage, etc.), finding pandan-flavored foods, and the occasional rainy days. I currently have Do What I Want by Kid Cudi and Ted Lasso (Apple TV+) on repeat. The last book I read was Crying in H Mart by Michelle Zauner.

How can I contact you?

Send an email to ppaulinanguyen at gmail.com. I usually reply within 3 business days, unless your message ends up in my spam folder or I have limited internet access.
