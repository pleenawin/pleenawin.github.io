Kauai, Hawaii

I traveled to Kauai for a long weekend where I ate a lot of really great food and saw breath-taking scenery. However, I don't recommend visiting during the rainy season.

day 1: the fish express

as the very first meal, it was only appropriate that poke would be it. i went to the fish express per a friend's recommendation, and it didn't disappoint! with plenty of options to choose from and DIY toppings, this cozy place packs great flavor in a small bowl. You should try the spicy ahi poke. Also, don't mind the random wild chickens around the island.

day 2: art cafe hemingway

the moment you step into this little cafe, you're immediately transported into another world. with hemingway-inspired decor, i'm sure he would have approved the food served here. You should try eggs benedict hemingway and moroccan iced tea.

day 3: waimea canyon

dubbed "the grand canyon of the pacific." if a picture is worth 1000 words, then take a look at the pictures :) pro-tip: go straight to the waimea canyon lookout if you aren't much of a hiker.

day 4: kauai coffee company

a native coffee company based in the southside of the island offers free tours at their plantation. you learn about how the kauai coffee company grow their coffee beans, how it's distributed, and after all that learning, you get (unlimited) free samples of their coffee! p.s. don't store your coffee in the freezer/fridge. You should try the banana nut creme coffee.

day 5: java kai

a small breakfast place serving up interesting plates with punchy coffee -- not to mention funny names e.g. crois-sean-wich. java kai is quite popular amongst the locals. great for groups, but watch out for those lines after 9am! You should try tropical waffles and mac nut latte.
